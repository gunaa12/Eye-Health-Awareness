Screen Time Related

Well, there are many activities that can be done without a screen. For example, you can go outside - of course, with all the precautions required - to bike, jog, skateboard, etc. You could start a small garden in your own backyard if interested. Try new hobbies such as cooking, a new sport, or just do chores around the house. You could be productive by learning something new through the use of books or simply read for pleasure.

If the device use is absolutely necessary, then there are some preventive measures that can be taken to reduce eye strain. Some of them include, good posture, proper eye distance from the screen, and the 20-20-20 rule.

The 20-20-20 rule is a measure that can be taken in order to reduce eye strain. This infographic from the American Optometric Institute illustrates it:


The following bullet points and the illustration explain what proper "posture" and ergonomics at the desk are:

  • Keep Moniter an arm's length away.
  • Sit with a straight back.
  • Bend your knees at 90 degrees and put both feet flat on the ground.
  • Have 2-3 inches of space between thigh and chair.
  • Keep wrists straight.
  • Keep hands at elbow level.
  • Don't sit for too long at a time. Take breaks!
